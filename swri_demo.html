Athera — Structural Weathering Risk Index

Move the sliders to set a patient's intake profile, then compare how the status-quo additive model and the SWRI interaction model score the same patient.

How SWRI differs: Traditional risk models assume that factors such as COVID burden (C), psychosocial stress (s), environmental burden (E), and baseline vulnerability (B) contribute independently. This SWRI takes it further by evaluating whether these factors amplify one another when they occur together.

Weights fitted via logistic regression on 15,000 synthetic patients constructed to reflect literature-described syndemic interaction effects (not real patient data). C×S interaction significant at p<0.0001 (Wald test); C×E and S×E were directionally consistent but not statistically significant at this sample size — illustrating why larger real-world datasets matter for detecting structural interaction effects.
